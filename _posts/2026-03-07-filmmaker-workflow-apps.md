A good workflow means less time managing logistics and more time creating. Here's how to build an efficient filmmaker workflow with apps.

## The Workflow Stages

### 1. Concept & Writing
**Tool:** WriterSolo or Highland (free)
Write your screenplay in proper format. Export to PDF for sharing.

### 2. Planning
**Tools:** Google Sheets + ShotLister
Break your script into scenes. Create your shot list. Build your schedule. Share with your team.

### 3. Location & Lighting
**Tool:** Sun Seeker ($10)
Plan natural lighting angles for each location and time of day.

### 4. On Set
**Tools:** [FrameCoach](https://framecoach.io) + Blackmagic Camera
FrameCoach coaches camera decisions. Blackmagic Camera gives you manual control. Together they cover the technical side so you can focus on directing.

### 5. Data Management
**Tool:** Your file naming system
Name files consistently: `ProjectName_Scene01_Take03_CamA.mov`. Back up to two drives immediately after each shoot day.

### 6. Editing
**Tool:** DaVinci Resolve (free)
Import, organize into bins by scene, assemble → rough → fine cut.

### 7. Color & Sound
**Tool:** DaVinci Resolve (free)
Grade in the Color page. Mix in Fairlight. One app for everything.

### 8. Export & Delivery
**Tool:** DaVinci Resolve + Handbrake
Export master file from Resolve. Use Handbrake for web-optimized versions.

### 9. Distribution
**Tools:** YouTube + FilmFreeway
Upload to YouTube/Vimeo. Submit to festivals.

## The Key Principle

Fewer tools, mastered deeply, beats many tools used superficially. Pick one tool per stage and learn it well.
