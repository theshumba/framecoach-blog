As a director, your job is making creative decisions quickly and confidently. These tools support that.

## Decision Support
**[FrameCoach](https://framecoach.io)** — Your on-set camera coaching assistant. FrameCoach helps you make informed decisions about camera settings and composition in real-time, so you can focus on performance and story.

## Lens Visualization
**Artemis Director's Viewfinder** ($30) — Preview how different lenses will frame a shot from any position. Walk a location and plan every setup before bringing the camera.

**Cadrage** ($10) — Similar viewfinder functionality at a lower price point.

## Shot Reference
**ShotDeck** (free browse) — Search a massive library of film stills by shot type, angle, lighting, and film. Find reference images for your DP and share a visual language for the look you want.

## Storyboard Review
**Storyboarder** (free) — Review your storyboards on set. Compare what you planned to what you're getting.

## Actor Direction
Keep notes (paper or phone) on character motivations and scene beats. Between setups, review your notes instead of winging it. Prepared directors give better direction.

## Continuity
Take a photo of every setup with your phone. When you return for coverage or pickups, you can match exactly.

## The Director's Mindset
Tools support decisions but don't replace vision. Know what you want before looking at any app. The best directors use tools to execute their vision faster, not to figure out what that vision is.
